21 Day Follow-Up

Day 16

Memory

Memory Applications - Journeys

REVIEW - Yesterday you used your new 10 stop desk journey to memorise a work-related item such as a policy, procedure, mission statement, vision or values statement. Or even Australia's first 10 Prime Ministers.

Take a moment now and run through your journey and see if you can recall the key points in the right order.

Memory Applications - Names

REVIEW - Who did you meet yesterday? Create images for any new first names and add them to your Visual Vocabulary files.

Yesterday you downloaded the Visual Vocabulary file for recording surnames, then thought of images for the surnames of 5 work colleagues or friends.

Picture each of the 5 people and recall the image that you created for their surname and how you connected it to them.

Key Memory Skills - Observation And Visual Recall

Earlier in this follow-up you did a focus exercise where you actively observed an item then closed your eyes and recalled it in as much detail as possible.

We're now going to build on this by applying it to people, or more specifically, their faces. So for the next few days you'll start to closely observe people's individual facial features such as noses, ears, eyes and lips.

Doing this will force you to really pay attention to the people you meet and at the same time will develop your powers of observation and visual recall - key memory skills.

Today we'll start with lips. Here's what to do. When you meet people today, simply pay attention to their lips, noticing all the different shapes and sizes that you see.

Even though you are focusing on one individual facial feature you'll probably find that you can close your eyes and easily and clearly recall a person's face in a lot of detail.

And if you can create and recall a vivid mental image of a person's face then remembering their name is easy - you just add in a visual prompt, something that will remind you of their name. And by now you've already had lots of practice at that.

So for the rest of today pay special attention to everyone you meet, and in particular the shape, size, colour etc. of their lips. Have fun!

Information Mapping

Business Applications - Reports And Presentations

One of the key business tasks where information mapping can save you heaps of time is preparing reports and presentations. And if you use the GO technique - GENERATE then ORGANISE - you'll be able to develop and then communicate your key messages in a quick, clear and logical way.

Have a go at the following…

1) Pick a work-related topic on which you will have to write a report or deliver a presentation in the next 30 days. Or a personal one if you prefer.

2) Spend a moment to decide what you want your key message to be. What would be the single most important thing that you would like your reader or audience to take away? Once you are clear on this, write it down in one sentence - keep it short and simple!

3) Open up a map and type your key message in the centre. Now spend no more than 5 minutes to generate your content. As quickly as possible dump down all thoughts and ideas that come to mind. Aim for quantity at this stage and don't worry about relevance, order etc. Just generate and capture ideas.

Tomorrow you'll review and organise your map.

End of Day 16!
