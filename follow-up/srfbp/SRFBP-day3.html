Speed Reading For Business Professionals
7 Day Follow-Up Program

Day 3

Today's Activities

You know from the workshop that your brain can take in information in a fraction of a second. And in general much faster than at the pace at which we are used to reading.

So the challenge is simply to break out of our slow-reading habits by progressively forcing ourselves to develop new habits such as reading with a guide and reading at a faster pace. So let's have a go.

RocketReader
- Flash Exercise

Spend a few minutes practising the 'flash' exercise. This time deliberately start to push yourself. Either increase the speed at which the words flash up or increase the starting number of characters. Or both! Really focus and see how far you can progress and how fast you can actually take in written information.

Reading Practice
- Increasing Speed

As you know, a guide not only reduces back-skipping and keeps us focused but it can also be a very effective tool for pacing yourself to read faster. And that's the aim of today's reading practice.

Spend at least 5 minutes in total reading your chosen book or material. Read using a guide. For the initial half of the time, focus on getting in to a good comfortable reading pace and rhythm. Then for the second half of the time, use your guide to deliberately force yourself to read faster so that you really need to focus 100%.

It should be a little uncomfortable and a challenge - though if you lose comprehension and are no longer taking in the information when you are reading then you're probably going a little too fast at this stage.

Memory Techniques
- Number Rhyme

At the workshop we went through the Number Rhyme technique. Take a moment now to review your images for the numbers 1 to 10. See each one as clearly and in as much detail as you can.

Decide to use the technique at some point this week either at work or socially. Also explain the system to someone else - teaching others is a really great way of reinforcing your knowledge whatever the topic.

End of Day 3!
