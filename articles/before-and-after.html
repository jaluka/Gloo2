How To Remember Names
 

We've had a ball this month running heaps of in-house training for corporate clients.

One of the great things about working with people face-to-face is the 'A-ha!' moment that most people experience when they see how memory techniques really work in practice.

Another great thing is the questions that come up.

Earlier this week a lady asked

'What is your number 1 tip for remembering names?'

Great question.

And the answer may surprise you.

Now I could have mentioned the importance of paying attention and focusing on the person's name.

Or I could have talked about the use of the 5 simple memory techniques for making names stick.

Or the highly effective location method.

But no.

If I had to really focus in on how to remember names, to zero in on one single thing that really locks names into your long-term memory... and serves them back up when you need them... it is this -

*** REVIEW ***

Or in other words...

It's what you do AFTER you meet someone that makes the difference.

Now let's assume that you're already doing the right things - actively paying attention, focusing on what the person looks like and clearly hearing their name.

(You may also have begun to see how easy it is to create an image for somebody's name - and then link the image to the person.)

The next step is really simple.

Next time you really want to lock in someone's name,do the following:

1. Shortly after you meet them, picture their face clearly in your mind's eye and say their name out loud (if you created an image for their name see this too)

2. Later the same day do exactly the same thing.

3. The next day, you guessed it... do exactly the same thing.

Should take you no more than 10 seconds each time.

Just 10 seconds or less to avoid what will seem like an eternity of embarrassment when you bump into whatshisface in the supermarket next weekend.

Review works.

The simple act of reviewing any information you need to remember makes a big difference to the amount that you can recall.

Now I'm not the world's biggest fan of rote learning.

In fact, I have painful memories of sitting in a classroom and being forced to repeat my times tables ad nauseam.

Not fun... but at 36 years old I can still recall 7x9 (on a good day) so it clearly worked.

But combining memory techniques with effective review - that's a powerful formula for locking in names…or any information for that matter.

What constitutes effective review is a matter for debate.

Some people recommend very specific review cycles e.g. daily, weekly, monthly,
6 monthly etc. Which is great if you have the discipline to stick to it.

I've personally found that reviewing things briefly the same day, the next day and several days later is more than enough.

Everyone is different so see what works for you.

And finally... 1 extra tip for making your life easier when it comes to meeting groups of people.

Here's what I do.

Whenever I give a talk, do a seminar or run a training session I always request a list of the people attending and their names.

I then run through the list and create images for their names.

When I meet somebody and they tell me their name the image I have already created for them usually pops into my mind. I then just mentally link the person with the image.

Works a treat.

Admittedly, there are occasions where it's not possible to get this info in advance. But you'll also find that there are many instances where you can get a list of who'll be at the meeting... the training session... the event... the function.

Give it a go. You'll soon be unforgettable with names.

The Final Word: When it comes to remembering names, it's what you do BEFORE and AFTER that can make your life a lot easier.
