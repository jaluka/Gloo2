How To Remember More Of What You Read
 

Apparently the average person spends 24 years of their life asleep. Wow. That's a lot of time. And as you know, time is money.

Wouldn't it be great if you could read while asleep?

Sleep Reading. Imagine that.

Well... Gloo scientists have just made a remarkable new discovery... it is possible!

In fact, it happens every day. You may even have experienced the phenomenon yourself.

You open a book, you start to read, you get to the end of the page and... BLANK! You haven't got a clue what you just read.

Voilà - Sleep Reading!

You haven't got a clue what you just read because your mind was wandering...

... must remember to call whatshisname

... did I lock the front door?

... what am I doing tonight?

and of course

... what's for dinner?

The sad fact is that you will forget most of what you read... and waste a fair chunk of your precious time each and every week in the process.

So How Can You
Remember More Of What You Read?

The starting point is to be *Actively Involved* in the material you read.

And the good news is that if you are using the 3 speed reading tips we shared with you recently, then these will automatically force you to be more Actively Involved in the material that you read.

And by adding a few extra steps you can significantly increase what you recall using the following BDA Approach* for remembering what you read...

STEP 1: Before Reading

Pre-reading questions - before you read, ask yourself a series of questions such as;

* What's the main subject?
* Who is the author?
* When was the material written?
* What is your purpose for reading it?
* What do you currently know about the subject?
* Are there any specific questions you need answered?

Asking and answering a series of questions will engage your mind and give purpose and focus to your reading. If you are familiar with Information Mapping you may choose to Information Map these questions and your answers.

Preview the material - if it is a book, read what's on the back cover then look through the table of contents to get a feel for the material. Next, flick through the material page by page at high speed without stopping. Don't try to read the material - simply notice what catches your eye. Previewing builds familiarity which is key to understanding and remembering.

STEP 2: During Reading

Actively use speed reading techniques - apply speed reading techniques such as the use of a Visual Guide. Reading faster using these techniques requires more focus and concentration. This in turn aids remembering and recall.

Mark-up the material - as long as it is yours! As you read, highlight key points and write brief notes in the margins. This forces you to be more actively involved in what you are reading.

STEP 3: After Reading

Map the key points - one of the keys to remembering what you read is to make notes of the key points in your own words. Again, we recommend the use of Information Maps. A simple Information Map of the key points can be done very quickly and will serve as a powerful memory tool.

Review your notes - not the material! To boost your long-term retention and recall, review your notes the next day, the next week and the next month. Regular, brief review is a highly effective way to lock in what you need to remember.

The Final Word: There really is no secret to remembering what you read. Give the BDA Approach a go.

*BDA = Yep you guessed it, Before During After

 

Enjoy your dinner.
